**Access:** Public

Mint lets you create, explore, and share 3D spaces called Worlds. You can also
create 3D Models, capture real rooms and objects with the mobile app, browse
other creators' work, and use optional Live Room features.

This guide is written for people using Mint, not building it.

## What Mint is for

Mint is for creators, hobbyists, teams, and curious explorers who want to:

- Turn prompts and images into 3D spaces.
- Turn a prompt or image into a downloadable 3D Model.
- Browse public Worlds and creator profiles.
- Share a World with a link, embed it, or take screenshots.
- Use optional Live Room features with guests.

## What you can do in Mint

- Browse featured Worlds from the Home page.
- Search for Worlds by name.
- Open a World and move through it in 3D.
- Create a new World from Chat using text, images, Street View links, or supported listing links.
- Create a 3D Model from text or an image.
- Capture rooms, objects, and people with the Mint mobile app.
- Reuse an existing World by starting from its current look.
- Save Favorites and manage your public profile.
- Manage Credits, invite friends, and manage optional plans if they are available on your Account.

## Quick start

1. Open Mint and browse the Home page.
2. Click a World thumbnail to explore it.
3. Click **New chat** and sign in when you are ready to make something.
4. Describe a World or 3D Model, or add a reference image.
5. Review the Preview, then start the final generation.
6. Open your **Profile** or mobile **My Models** to find what you made.

Example:

```text
World prompt: A cozy mountain cabin at sunset with warm lights and pine trees
3D Model prompt: A stylized red scooter with chrome trim
```
